How to compute the probability of union of several events?

Nov 20, 2020 • Peiyi Hung • 1 min read

category   learning

The problem I intend to solve in this post is how to compute the probability of union of several events (not necessarily disjoint)?

Incusion-Exclusion

For any events $A_1$,...,$A_n$, we can compute the unoin of these events by

$$P(\bigcup_{i=1}^{n}A_i) = \sum_{i}P(A_{i}) - \sum_{i<j}P(A_{i}\cap A_{j}) + \sum_{i<j<k}P(A_{i}\cap A_{j}\cap A_{k}) - ...+ (-1)^{n+1} P(A_1\cap A_2\cap...\cap A_n)$$

When $n=2$, this formula will reduce to a special case which we are more fimiliar with:

$$P(A_{1} \cup A_{2}) = P(A_1) + P(A_2) - P(A_1 \cap A_2)$$

Let's see how we can use this formula in a problem.

Example problem

For a group of 7 people, find the probability that all 4 seasons (winter, spring, summer and fall) occur at least once each among their birthdays, assuming that all seasons are equally likely.

First, we define several events: $A_1$: there is no birthday in winter $A_2$: there is no birthday in spring

  • $A_3$: there is no birthday in summer
  • $A_4$: there is no birthday in fall

We know that $P(\text{all 4 seasons occur at least once})$ would be $1 - P(\text{at least one season has no birthday in it})$. So if we express this in event, that will be $$P(\text{all 4 seasons occur at least once}) = 1 - P(A_1 \cup A_2 \cup A_3 \cup A_4)$$

Next we can compute $P(A_1 \cup A_2 \cup A_3 \cup A_4)$ using the inclusion-exclusion formula. Before we compute our union probablity, we need to calculate some probabilities of intersection of events:

  • $P(A_1) = \frac{3^7}{4^7} $
  • $P(A_1 \cap A_2) = \frac{2^7}{4^7}$
  • $P(A_1 \cap A_2 \cap A_2) = \frac{1}{4^7}$

By symmetry and inclusion-exclusion, we have

$$P(A_1 \cup A_2 \cup A_3 \cup A_4) = 4 \times \frac{3^7}{4^7} - 6 \times \frac{2^7}{4^7} + 4\times\frac{1}{4^7}$$

Therefore, $P(\text{all 4 seasons occur at least once}) = 1 - 4\frac{3^7}{4^7} - 6 \frac{1}{2^7} + \frac{4}{4^7} \approx 0.513$
